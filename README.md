# The Seven Riddles of Yevamot 97b

An illustrated walkthrough of the seven cryptic family-relationship riddles preserved in **Talmud Bavli, Yevamot 97b** — each presented with its Aramaic source, English translation, an SVG genealogy diagram, and a short halakhic explainer.

**Live page:** https://ezrabrand.github.io/yevamot-97b-riddles/

## What's inside

A single self-contained `index.html` (no build step, no dependencies beyond a Google Fonts link) covering:

1. *"My half-brother is the husband of my mother"* — the only riddle solvable in a permitted manner under the Rabbis (against R' Yehuda).
2. *"He is my brother and he is my son"* — a non-Jew with his daughter.
3. *"I am the daughter of your sister"* — a non-Jew with his daughter's daughter.
4. *"I am the daughter of his brother"* — a non-Jew with his son's daughter.
5. *"Woe, woe for my brother, who is my father, and my husband..."* — three stacked incestuous unions; the most tangled riddle in the sugya.
6. *"You and I are siblings; your father and I are siblings; your mother and I are siblings"* — one woman holds three sibling-relationships at once.
7. *"You and I are cousins..."* — the second riddle with a permitted solution, via three brothers' intermarrying households.

## Diagram conventions

- Square = male, circle = female (standard genealogy).
- Gold ring = the speaker of the riddle.
- Dashed red lines = incestuous unions.

## Source

Hebrew/Aramaic text and English translation adapted from the Koren-Steinsaltz tradition. Diagrams hand-composed in SVG.
